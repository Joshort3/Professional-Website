About Me
Howdy,
I'm Joshua Ortiz and I'm studying Computer Engineering at Texas A&M University. My passion is programming. I love writing programs and seeing my completed products. My dream to help push computer technology further and develop programs that all people can use to help with their daily lives. I'm involved with Texas A&M Esports program. I compete almost every Friday at their weekly Super Smash Tournaments. I'm not the best player, but nonetheless I have fun competing.

Background
I was born in Brooklyn, New York, but lived in Little Elm, Texas for most of my life. I am second generation Hispanic American. My mother is from Colombia and father is Puerto Rican, so I'm a mixture two amazing cultures. I'm also the youngest brother of 3 and we're all first-generation college students. To the left is a picture of my brother and me visiting New York City.
Life in High School
My high school was called Little Elm High School and our mascot was the Lobo. I was proud member of our Lobo Marching Band playing the Tenor Saxophone for all 4 years. I was also in 3 clubs, National Honor Society, Spanish National Honor Society, and Jazz Band. Science was my favorite category in school, so by graduation I had taken 7 science courses. At the end of high school, I graduated top 15th in my class.
